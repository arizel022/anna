Largo

What requirements does culture impose on a modern man?

Is the question of which man to be a gender or values problem?

What are the recognizable traits of male portraying and behavior in modern society?

Male characters exist in art history for several thousand years, but each new era imposes new standards on us, which connect the ideas of "Golden ratio" in antiquity with inexorable tendencies of time.

  • The male body was never an object of such obsession as a female one, but thanks to Christianity and ancient Greece legacy, it's instantly grasped and recognized in European culture. Policlet's "Kanon" is replaced by a huge role of Crucifixion, which takes a central place in the iconography of Europe â€“ the image of warrior and victim were associated with the male nature. In parallel with the Eurocentric view of the world non-European peoples developed their own visual tradition - the human body acquired an important role in the African, Indian and Australian culture: depiction of the physique becomes an important part of the national ethnic art. Man is cut out of ivory and stone, his image should respond to the idea of masculinity and society assigned male features.

    Contemporary photographers are actively working with the ideas of the male body and posing. Questions about the essence of masculinity are asked in works of many artists; for them male body is a tool in search of their own identity.

  • Photos of various men, made in imitation of the Renaissance tradition, ancient sculpture and biblical images is the series in which Hanna Melnykova examines matters of masculinity. Mantegna and Giotto's allusions and quotations, the Crucifixion and the sculptures of neoclassicism, baroque poses and classical draperies are obvious in this project. It is also a binary work: a female perspective on man as an object and a man's view of himself as a subject at the same time. What is the man-model thinking posing for a female photographer? About the preservation of his gender role or according to public stereotypes? About how a woman sees him or whether he can match the representations of himself?

    Psychological profiles in which the faces are rarely recognized but poses and emotions are usually read transfer anonymity and complexity of male identification when visual tradition turned into information noise. Preserving the common features but being something elusive and usurped by media, contemporary masculine ideal eludes our eyes, embodying a dream, which has it so difficult to become a reality.
